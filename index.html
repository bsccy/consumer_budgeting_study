Participant Information and Consent Form

You are invited to take part in a research study about personal budgeting under changing economic conditions. The purpose of this study is to understand how people make budgeting decisions when the prices of consumer goods and services change over time.

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to complete an online study that includes a short set of questions and a budgeting task. The study will take approximately 8 to 15 minutes to complete. You will be asked to respond based on your own experience and judgment.

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may choose not to take part, or to withdraw at any time before submitting your responses, without penalty or loss of any benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

There are no anticipated risks beyond those encountered in everyday life. Your responses will be anonymous and confidential. No directly identifying personal information will be collected. All data collected will be used for research purposes only.

By clicking “I Agree” below, you confirm that you have read and understood the information above and voluntarily consent to take part in this study.

Task Overview

Thank you for taking part in this study.

In this task, you will be asked to imagine your own monthly budgeting decisions under a series of simulated economic conditions. In each round, you will see information about the current economic environment and the amount of money available to you for that month. You will then decide how much to allocate to different spending categories.

Please answer as realistically as possible, based on how you think you would respond in such a situation. There are no right or wrong answers. We are interested in your personal judgments and decisions.

Important: you do not have to allocate all available money. You may leave some money unallocated in your current account if that is what you think you would do.

Current account and carry-over rule

Any money you leave unallocated at the end of a round remains in your current account and carries over to the next round.

In each round, your available budget is:

Base monthly income + money carried over from the previous round = available budget this round
Long-term Savings & Investment is locked.
Money allocated to this category is treated as committed. It does not come back as carry-over in the next round.

You may not allocate more than the available budget shown for that round.

Category ratings

Before the budgeting task, please rate each spending category.

Please rate how essential each category is for maintaining a normal monthly standard of living, how easy it would be to reduce for one month if necessary, and the minimum amount that would still be acceptable for one month.
For the Essentialness and Flexibility scales, please click on each scale to activate it before selecting a value. The scale will not show a pre-selected value until you click it.

Budgeting task

In this task, you will complete four monthly budgeting rounds under changing economic conditions.

What is inflation? Inflation refers to a general increase in prices over time. When inflation is higher, the same amount of money may buy fewer goods and services than before. In this task, information about inflation is used to represent changes in the wider economic environment.

In each round, you will see information about the current economic environment. You should use this information, together with your own judgment and experience, to decide how you would allocate your money across the spending categories shown.

Please think about how you would budget for yourself under each set of conditions. We are interested in how you adjust your budget in response to these changing conditions. There are no right or wrong answers.

  • Base monthly income each round: £5,000
  • Unused money remains in your current account and carries over to the next round
  • Total allocated must not exceed the available budget shown for that round
